Plant & Crop Stress Physiology

About my work

I am interested in how plant structure and function interact to determine how crops respond to changing environments. My research focuses on how vegetable crops respond to water stress and other abiotic drivers, as well as biotic stressors such as common diseases. Much of my work is centered on lettuce, tomato, and other vegetable crops in California’s Central Coast, where drought, salinity, heat, and disease all converge in real production systems.
