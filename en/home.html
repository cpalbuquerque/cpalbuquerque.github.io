Crop Stress Physiology

Plant physiology applied to vegetable cropping systems in California’s Salinas Valley and Central Coast.

About my research

My research interests are focused on using plant physiology applied to crops, relating plant structure and function to understand how we can optimize water and nutrient use efficiency, with a special focus on crops of the Salinas Valley.

I work at the interface of fundamental plant stress physiology and applied agronomy to address real challenges in vegetable production, including drought, salinity, heat, and disease. By integrating field experiments, controlled environment studies, and quantitative analyses, my goal is to support more resilient and sustainable vegetable cropping systems.
