Communication & Outreach

Working with growers & industry

My outreach focuses on building two-way conversations between research and practice in vegetable cropping systems. I work with growers, PCAs, advisors, and industry scientists in California’s Central Coast to discuss irrigation, water quality, salinity, and crop stress, and to co-design field trials that address practical questions in lettuce, tomato, and other vegetable crops.

Crop Irrigation School

The Crop Irrigation School is a collaborative effort to share practical, science-based information on irrigation management for vegetable crops in the Central Coast and beyond.

Field demonstrations, talks, and hands-on sessions on topics such as sensor-based irrigation scheduling, salinity management, and water quality, developed in collaboration with growers and partners.

Selected outreach activities

  • Presentations and discussions with growers and industry partners on crop water use, salinity, and stress physiology.
  • Field days and tours in collaboration with Central Coast partners to showcase irrigation and soil management strategies.
  • Mentoring undergraduate students in projects that connect lab and field measurements to grower questions.
