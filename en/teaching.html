Teaching & Mentoring

Teaching at California State University, Monterey Bay

My teaching focuses on connecting plant ecophysiology and agronomy to the realities of Central Coast agriculture. Students work with real datasets, field sites, and local partners from the Salinas Valley to understand how water, soil, climate, and management shape vegetable cropping systems.

Courses

I teach undergraduate courses in plant ecophysiology, crop and soil research methods, and irrigation management. Course content integrates lecture, discussion, lab, and field-based activities whenever possible.

BIO 332
Plant Ecophysiology
How plants respond to environmental drivers such as water availability, light, and temperature, with emphasis on crop and natural systems in California. Labs introduce core physiological measurements and data analysis.
AGPS 350
Research Methods for Crop & Soil Science
Experimental design, field and lab methods, and statistical analysis using datasets from vegetable production systems. Students design small projects relevant to Salinas Valley agriculture.
AGPS 415
Advanced Irrigation & Management
Principles and practices of irrigation for crop production, including scheduling, sensor technologies, and regulatory constraints. Emphasis on lettuce and other vegetable crops in coastal and inland California.

Mentoring

Undergraduate students are central to my research and outreach activities. They contribute to lab and field projects on lettuce, tomato, soil–water relations, and biochar, and present their work at regional conferences.

  • Mentoring multiple undergraduate research students at CSUMB in vegetable cropping systems.
  • Student projects span plant physiology, irrigation management, soil amendments, and disease stress.
  • Students participate in poster presentations and engage with growers and industry partners.
  • Co-mentoring master’s students in collaboration with the Scoffoni Lab.
