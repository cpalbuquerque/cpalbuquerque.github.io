Teaching & Mentoring

How I teach

In my classes I work to connect fundamental concepts in plant physiology with the complexity of real agricultural systems. I bring examples from lettuce, tomato, and other crops that students see in the Salinas Valley and use those systems to explore how plants move water, respond to stress, and allocate resources. I rely heavily on hands-on learning, curated datasets, and discussions around figures and graphs so that students practice interpreting evidence rather than just memorizing terms. My goal is for students to leave with a strong conceptual framework and the confidence to apply it in different crops, environments, and career paths.

I also draw on evidence-based pedagogy – mixing short lectures with think–pair–share, group problem solving, and reflective assignments – to create a classroom where students feel comfortable asking questions, making mistakes, and building understanding together.
